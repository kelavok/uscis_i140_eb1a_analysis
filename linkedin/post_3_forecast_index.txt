Can we estimate how current E11 and NIW pending inventory may eventually resolve?

I built an exploratory forecast by combining two signals.

First, annual USCIS snapshots show how pending inventory in the parent categories EB1 and EB2 matured into approvals and denials.

Second, quarterly RADP data shows how E11 differs from EB1 and how NIW differs from EB2. Across the available quarters, E11 denial odds were about 2.31 times the EB1 parent odds. NIW denial odds were about 2.60 times the EB2 parent odds.

The model combines those components in log-odds space:

program pending denial odds
= parent pending-resolution odds trend
x program-versus-parent odds multiplier trend

For the latest available period, FY2026 Q1, the model estimates:

• E11 pending-to-denial share: 61.6%
  Approximate 95% interval: 44.5%-76.2%

• NIW pending-to-denial share: 73.8%
  Approximate 95% interval: 52.7%-87.7%

For context, the observed denial share among already decided FY2026 Q1 cases was 52.5% for E11 and 57.4% for NIW.

These numbers are deliberately presented as ranges. They are not individual case probabilities, and they are not a claim that every pending petition belongs to the same filing cohort. USCIS does not provide direct E11/NIW cohort tracking, RFE history, premium-processing status, or adjudicator-level controls in these aggregate files.

The useful takeaway is not a single precise percentage. It is the direction and size of the correction: historical pending cases resolved less favorably than early decisions, while E11 and NIW currently show materially higher denial odds than their parent groups.

The model should be recalculated after every new USCIS quarter and backtested as later snapshots mature.
